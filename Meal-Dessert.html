Zucchini–Kuchen

(dessert)
  • 3 eggs
  • 250 g brown sugar
  • 150 g oil
  • 250 g flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda (natron)
  • 2 tsp baking powder (backpulver)
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 200 g ground hazelnuts
  • 300 g zucchini grated
  • choclate for coating
  1. Stir sugar, oil and eggs until foamy. Combine flour, Natron, Backpulver and cinnamon. Add ground hazelnuts and mix well. Combine dry and wet ingredients and stir well. Last add the grated zucchini and mix everything well.
  2. Spread on baking tray or into baking form.
  3. Bake at 180°C for approx. 40 minutes

Cheesecake

(Central European; dessert)
  • 500g regular quark (20% fat)
  • 3 eggs
  • 50g flour
  • 125g margarine
  • 105g sugar
  • 4g baking powder
  • 1 pkg vanilla sugar
  • lemon juice to taste
  1. Heat oven to 170°C
  2. Butter a 20 cm in diameter springform pan
  3. Whisk all ingredients together and pour into form
  4. Bake for 60 min
  5. Let cool down in the oven

Apple crumble pie

(dessert)
  • 3 apples
  • 200 g flour
  • 125 g butter
  • 100 g oats
  • 100 g sugar
  • pinch of salt
  • cinnamon
  1. Combine flower, oats, sugar and salt in a bowl
  2. Add cold butter cut up in dices, work quickly and pinch the dices with the dry ingredients to form bigger crums
  3. Peel, core and slice the apples
  4. Place sliced apples in a generously buttered baking form
  5. Sprinkle the apples with cinnamon (~2 tsps) and sugar (~2 Tbsps)
  6. Pour crumbled dough on top of the apples
  7. Bake 20-25 min on 225°C

Vanilla custard

(dessert)
  • 0.5 L milk
  • 1 vanilla pod
  • 50 g sugar
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 20 g starch
  1. Deseed and soak vanilla pod in milk over night
  2. Beat eggs and add sugar and starch in a pan
  3. Whisk in a bit of milk and put the pan on medium heat
  4. Bring up to 82°C (eggs starts cooking after the point)
  5. Check consistency by running finger over the back of wooden spoon, if the sauce stays separated, then it's good
  6. Cool down, and refrigerate

Mascarpone Amaretti mousse

(Italian; dessert)
  • 500g mascarpone
  • 200ml cream
  • 115g powderded sugar
  • 165g Amaretti coarsely crushed plus more for garnish
  • 33ml Amaretto
  1. Whip the cream.
  2. Mix mascarpone with powdered, crushed Amaretti and Amaretto.
  3. Carefully fold in the cream cream.
  4. Serve immediately or keep in the fridge until serving.

Savoy cabbage potatoe gratin

(Central European; dessert)
  • 1 Savoy cabbage (ca. 1,5 - 2 kg)
  • 1,5 - 2 kg potatoes, preferably waxy (equal amounts of cabbage and potatoes)
  • 1 big onion
  • 250 ml Cream (plant or dairy)
  • Ca. 500 ml Milk (plant or dairy)
  • 2 Tbsp Butter
  • 2 Tbsp flour
  • Breadcrumbs
  • Cheese (optional)
  • Chili pepper (optional)
  • Apple cider vinegar
  • Smokey salt
  1. Peel the potatoes and cut into slices.
  2. Cook potatoes until they are firm-tender (about 5-7 minutes).
  3. Fry the onion in a big pot, add cabbage. Cook, stirring occasionally, and scraping up any browned bits on the bottom of the pan, until vegetables have wilted and begun to brown, about 15 minutes.
  4. Add apple cider vinegar and deglaze pan, stirring rapidly and scraping up any browned bits stuck to the bottom.
  5. Add potatoes to cabbage mixture and stir to combine. Season thoroughly with Smokey salt and pepper.
  6. Make a roux, add the milk and cream and bring to a boil so it thickens. Season with salt.
  7. Add the sauce to the cabbage potatoe mixture and stir to combine.
  8. Coat an oven pan with butter and add the vegetable mix.
  9. Sprinkle breadcrumbs on top and cheese if desired.
  10. Bake around 30 minutes at around 200 C.
